# Homepage Copy Refresh

## Goal

Refresh the `undef games` public-site copy so a new visitor can understand the
company quickly, click into a real project immediately, and still feel the
distinctive scanline/station identity around the page.

This is a copy and content-structure refresh, not a visual redesign.

## Why This Needs To Change

The current homepage and adjacent pages have a stronger visual system than
verbal system. The interaction language and scanline identity are carrying too
much of the explanatory load, while the copy remains too abstract, too generic,
or too weakly differentiated between company, product, and atmosphere.

Right now the site does not cleanly answer three first-visit questions:

1. What is `undef games`?
2. What should I click first?
3. Why do these projects belong together?

The refresh should solve those questions without flattening the brand voice into
generic startup copy.

## Positioning

`undef games` should present itself as:

- an indie developer
- building strong game tools and systems
- to support fun experiences that bring people together
- online and off

The copy should support both product discovery and company understanding, in
that order:

1. click into a project
2. get a feel for the company/world
3. understand the company clearly
4. optionally follow for updates

## Audience

The homepage is optimized first for new visitors who do not already know the
company.

Those visitors may include:

- players
- hobbyists
- tabletop users
- technically curious game people
- early community members
- potential collaborators

The page is not primarily a contract-work pitch, consultancy pitch, or abstract
brand mood board.

## Product Hierarchy

### Flagship

`TradeWars: WARP Agent Runtime Platform` is the flagship.

It should read as:

- live
- clickably real
- actively developing
- early-stage in a positive way

### Secondary routes

`Undef Dice` and `Taybols` remain homepage routes and should read as concrete
products/projects rather than placeholder names.

- `Undef Dice` should feel fast, clear, social, and approachable
- `Taybols` should feel smaller-scale, stranger, and more experimental without
  sounding unserious or throwaway

### Not front-page primary products

`Undef Engine` and `Undef Sprite` should not be promoted as primary homepage
products for now.

They may inform tone and internal understanding, but they should not compete
with WARP, Dice, and Taybols on the homepage.

## Voice And Tone

The copy should be:

- direct
- technically credible
- human
- restrained

It should not be:

- over-rhetorical
- manifesto-heavy
- self-important
- overloaded with ‚Äúsignal/network/transmission‚Äù metaphors
- padded with startup filler

### Hard constraints

Avoid:

- inflated claims
- repeated rhetorical flourishes
- decorative metaphor doing explanatory work
- words like `innovation`, `immersive`, `redefine`, and similar generic filler

The station/BBS/scanline language can stay as framing texture, but it should
not be the primary substance of the message.

## Hero Direction

The current top hero composition is mostly correct and should remain largely
intact.

### Keep

- `undef games` as the primary hero anchor
- the existing visual identity and scanline/station atmosphere
- direct project routing near the top

### Change

- tighten the spacing between the header and hero slightly
- replace the support copy around the hero
- make the company explanation sharper

### Hero copy strategy

Do not replace the hero with a large slogan.

Do not use a long explanatory subhead.

Instead:

- keep the title simple and visually strong
- split explanation between a short support line and a compact paragraph
- make that paragraph answer what the company is and what kind of experiences
  it wants to support

## Copy Architecture

### 1. Hero support copy

The hero support copy should:

- define the company plainly
- make the technical orientation legible
- connect that technical orientation to shared play

It should sound like an indie developer with strong systems instincts, not a
tool vendor and not a vague arts label.

### 2. Project index

The project index should be the first actionable layer of the homepage.

It should read as:

- a focused product index with personality
- secondarily, a strange playable network directory

That means:

- WARP first
- Dice second
- Taybols third
- short project summaries that explain why each route exists

### 3. Company explanation

A later homepage section should explain how the projects fit together:

`undef games` builds software, systems, and play tools that help people gather,
operate, and play together across digital and physical settings.

This section should be short and plain.

### 4. Follow/update layer

The site may invite visitors to keep up with the company, but that should not
lead the page.

Updates/follow behavior comes after the company and project story are clear.

## Language System

Use varied but related phrasing instead of repeating a single line everywhere.

Approved thematic phrases include:

- `online and off`
- `digital and physical`
- `at the table and on the network`

These should be distributed across the site rather than stacked into a single
paragraph.

## Navigation Copy

Rename:

- `Blog` -> `Logs`

Why:

- it fits the BBS/station flavor
- it stays legible to normal visitors
- it is cleaner and less costume-like than `Bulletins` or `SysOp Log`

## CTA Direction

For WARP:

- prefer short CTA language
- keep the product expansion in adjacent copy, not inside the button itself

Recommended:

- CTA/button: `Explore WARP`
- supporting label/body: `TradeWars: WARP Agent Runtime Platform`

This preserves clarity without turning controls into long sentence fragments.

## Scope

This refresh should cover:

- homepage hero support copy
- homepage section kickers/headlines/body copy
- product summaries for WARP, Undef Dice, Taybols
- homepage explanatory company copy
- nav label change from `Blog` to `Logs`
- intro copy on `Games`, `Logs`, and `About` so the tone is consistent

This refresh should not require:

- a new information architecture
- a redesign of the top hero composition
- new product areas for engine/sprite
- a broader brand rewrite across unrelated repositories

## Content Rules By Surface

### Homepage

Priority:

1. project routes
2. atmosphere/personality
3. company explanation
4. updates/following

### Games page

Should read like a concrete index of active product surfaces, not a vague
landing page clone.

### Logs page

Should read like a place for development notes, release notes, and project
updates, with the BBS-era naming flavor but plain present-day language.

### About page

Should explain the company simply:

- who it is
- what it builds
- why the products belong together

## Success Criteria

The refresh succeeds if a new visitor can quickly infer:

1. `undef games` is an indie developer
2. the company builds game tools and systems in service of shared play
3. WARP is the flagship route
4. Undef Dice and Taybols are real and distinct follow-on routes
5. the brand feels distinctive without needing abstract copy to prop it up

## Implementation Notes

When implementation begins:

- preserve the strongest existing visual hierarchy
- adjust header/hero spacing slightly, not drastically
- prefer rewriting existing text over adding more text
- test both homepage and supporting pages for tone consistency
- verify that CTA/button copy stays short enough for the existing layout
